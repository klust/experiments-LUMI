compiler flags are added automatically. -craype-verbose will also show you
which options are added by the wrappers.

There is also some material for a 1-day course that we are developing already
(informatlly) available
on https://klust.github.io/LUMI-training-materials/1day-20230321/02_CPE/
though that isn't really that much GPU-oriented already.

an interactive session on a (part of) a GPU node. Ths standard version of ROCm
(the one that is supported by HPE) is actually different on the login nodes at
the moment and on the compute nodes, but that should be corrected in the
maintenance that's coming up at the end of next week.

Kurt
